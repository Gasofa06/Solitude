Privacy Concerns in Online Information Retrieval

When you search for online information, traditional servers might capture and analyze your queries, potentially compromising your privacy. Indeed, to ensure that only legitimate users can securely access their data, it's necessary to encrypt the information before transmitting it and refrain from sharing any secret key that enables its decryption.

However, an unfortunate drawback arises — while data encryption can effectively prevent unauthorized access, it also entails the destruction of the underlying semantic structure of the data, which makes it impossible to perform operations on them. As a result, the outcomes of operations on encrypted messages lack meaning when traditional encryption methods are used. This requires that, in either case, online services must decrypt the message before performing operations on them, as otherwise the result will have no meaning.

This need to decrypt the message before processing, allows online service providers to collect information about our searches.
